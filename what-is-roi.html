What Is ROI?

ROI (Return on Investment) is a financial metric used to measure the profitability of an investment. It helps businesses and investors evaluate how efficiently their capital is being used.

ROI Formula

The ROI formula is:
ROI = (Net Profit / Investment Cost) × 100%

Why ROI Is Important

  • Measures business performance
  • Helps compare different investments
  • Used in marketing, startups, real estate

ROI Example

If you invest $1,000 and earn $1,300, your net profit is $300. Your ROI would be 30%.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does ROI mean?

ROI stands for Return on Investment. It shows how profitable an investment is compared to its cost.

What is a good ROI?

A good ROI depends on the industry. Generally, an ROI above 15–20% is considered good.

How do you calculate ROI?

ROI is calculated using the formula: (Net Profit ÷ Investment Cost) × 100%.

Is ROI useful for small businesses?

Yes, ROI is widely used by startups and small businesses to measure efficiency.
